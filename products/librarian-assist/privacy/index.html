Privacy Policy

Librarian Assist — an Android app by su-do.

Last updated: 23 July 2026

Librarian Assist ("the app") is developed by su-do ("we", "us"). This page explains what data the app collects, how it's stored, and what control you have over it. The app is built for librarians and library staff managing their library's books, members and records.

Data stored on your device

Librarian Assist is offline-first: your library's data is stored in a local database on the device it's installed on, and we never receive a copy of it on our own servers. Depending on how you use the app, this local database can include:

  • Book catalogue records (title, author, category, price, shelf, barcode, etc.)
  • Member records you enter — name, date of birth, phone number, address, gender, email, blood group, job, ward and membership type
  • Lending / in-out register entries linking members to books
  • Event records, including any photos you attach
  • Finance records — income, expenses and membership fee collections

Member and financial data entered into the app is provided by you (the librarian) about your library's members — you control what's entered, exported or deleted. Since this data lives on the device and, if you enable backup, in your own Google Drive, su-do does not have access to it.

Google Sign-In & Drive backup (optional)

Backup is off by default. If you choose to sign in with Google, the app requests only the drive.file scope — access limited to files the app itself creates in your Google Drive. Using this feature, the app can:

  • Create a backup file of your local database in a folder in your own Drive
  • List and restore your own previous backups from that folder
  • Export your data to a Google Sheet in your own Drive, if you use that feature

The app cannot see or access any other files in your Google Drive. Your Google account credentials are handled entirely by Google's sign-in flow — we never see your password. You can revoke the app's access at any time from your Google Account permissions page.

Advertising

Librarian Assist shows banner ads served by Google AdMob to support free use of the app. AdMob may collect device identifiers (such as your advertising ID), approximate location and app usage data to serve and measure ads, as described in Google's Partner Sites policy. Where required (for example, under GDPR in the EEA/UK), the app shows a consent form before loading any ads, and your choice is respected for future sessions.

Permissions

The app requests device permissions only for features you actively use: file access to import/export Excel workbooks and share exported Word/Excel files, and network access for optional Google sign-in, Drive backup, and ad delivery. No permission is used to collect data beyond what's described on this page.

Data retention & deletion

Your library's data stays on your device until you delete it — either from within the app or by uninstalling it, which removes the local database entirely. If you've enabled Drive backup, backup files remain in your own Google Drive under your control; deleting them there, or revoking the app's Drive access, removes them from Google's systems according to Google's own retention practices — we hold no separate copy.

Children's privacy

Librarian Assist is a professional tool for library staff and is not directed at children. Any member records entered (which may relate to library members of any age) are managed entirely by the librarian using the app, under their own responsibility as the data controller for their library's records.

Changes to this policy

If this policy changes, we'll update the "Last updated" date above and, for material changes, note it on the Librarian Assist product page.
